Anki: Quick Start Guide

Introduction

Anki is a free and powerful tool for studying with flashcards using spaced repetition. Spaced repetition is a method which shows newer or harder content more frequently, while content which is older or easier is shown less frequently. Anki is known to be especially popular for medical students or language learning. However, any student, in any area of study, can benefit from using Anki to efficiently commit information to long-term memory. This guide will explain the steps necessary to set up the app and to integrate it into a daily study routine.

Requirements

In order to run Anki, you will need a personal computer with a recent version of Windows, macOS, or Linux. For specific version requirements, refer to the following list:

  • Windows 10 or 11 (64 bit)
  • macOS 10.13.4 or later
  • A recent Intel/AMD Linux distro (64 bit)

This guide is written for Anki Qt6, which is the recommended variant for most users.

Set Up

Step 1: Install Anki

1.1: Download the installer for the latest version of Anki Qt6 for your operating system from the Anki download page. 1.2: Run the installer. You will be prompted to choose a destination folder. Choosing a folder is optional. If you are unsure, press "Install" to continue with the default destination. Wait for the program to finish installing. You may close the installer when you see "Completed".

(1.1) Download Page

Step 2: Open Anki

Open the installation of Anki which you have just installed. You should see a window with the main interface for Anki, as seen in the adjacent image.

(2) Anki Window

Step 3: Create a Deck

A deck is a collection of flashcards. You may choose to create a separate deck per subject, or create a single deck for all of your flashcards. To begin, create a deck by pressing "Create Deck" at the bottom of the window. You will be prompted to enter a name for your deck. Enter an appropriate name for the deck. You will be able to change it later. After creating your deck, you should see it listed under "Deck".

(3) Create Deck

Step 4: Add a Card

Press the "Add" button at the top of the window. A new window will pop up. The most important functions are labeled in the adjacent image. First, note the "Type" function (1). Make sure the card type is set to "Basic". The "Basic" type is the most similar to a traditional flashcard. Next, select the deck which you created in the previous step (2). Then, enter the content of your card. For a basic card, enter a question or definition in the "Front" section (3). Enter the answer or associated term in the "Back" section (4). Finally, press "Add" (5) to create the card.

(4) Add a New Card

Step 5: Add More Cards

Repeat Step 4 whenever you encounter a new term or idea which you wish to commit to your memory. This step should be performed whenever you engage with new content which you intend to memorize (e.g. notes from a lecture or a book).

Study Your Deck

Step 6: Start Studying a Deck

Choose a deck and click on it. Ideally, you should check your deck(s) every day for new terms to review. You should see a window displaying how many cards are in "New", "Learning", or "Review". Anki will automatically set the status of a card based on your performance. Press "Study Now" to begin a session.

(6) Opening a Deck

Step 7: Recall

Anki will show you cards which you are due to study. For each card, you will see its front content. Try to recall the associated answer or term. When you recall it, or fail to recall it, press either the "Show Answer" button or the spacebar to show the answer.

(7) Card Front

Step 8: Record Your Performance

You should now see the answer for the card, along with a set of choices at the bottom of the window. If you completely failed to recall the card, press "Again". If you took a very long time to recall the answer, or made a minor mistake in your answer, choose "Hard". If you took a reasonable amount of time to recall the answer, choose "Good". If you recalled the answer very fast or instantly, choose "Easy".

(8) Card Back

Step 9: Finish the Session

You will see a message when you run out of cards to review. At this point, you have successfully studied this deck for the day. Ideally, repeat steps 6-9 every day in order for spaced repetition to be effective.

(9) Deck Finished

Step 10: Change Options as Necessary (Optional)

If you find that the number of cards you are reviewing per day or that the spacing between cards is ineffective, you can adjust these options for a given deck. Find the deck you wish to configure, and hover over it with your cursor. You should see a settings wheel to the right of the deck's name. Press the settings wheel and press "Options" to change any intervals or limits as desired.
